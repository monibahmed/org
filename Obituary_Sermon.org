Sign of Allah – Hadhrat Chaudhry Muhammad Zafarullah Khan Sahib (ra)
Eulogizing Friday Sermon delivered on September 6, 1985 in Bait-ul-Fazal, London
By Hadhrat Mirza Tahir Ahmad Khalifat-ul-Masih IV (rh)
 
After the customary opening prayers and recitation of Surah Fatiha, Hudhur recited the following verses of the Holy Quran:
(Al-Kahf: 108 – 111)
 
And then stated: 

The verses of the Holy Quran that I have recited have
been taken from Surah Al-Kahf and are the last few verses of the
chapter. From these verses, the last three verses do not seem to have
a common subject. In these three verses, there seems to be no relevance from
the subject matter of one verse to the next. One who views them with a
cursory glance supposes that each verse deals with a completely
different subject. However, the reality is that there is one
consistent subject with a deep interconnection.

The first verse mentions the believers, those who believed and
performed good deeds (Arabic text: kaanat lahum janaat al-firdos…)
Allah will grant gardens of paradise as a hospitality for
them. (Arabic Text: khalidina feeha laa yabghoona anha hiwala…) They
will remain in them forever and will never be separated from
them. (Arabic text: yab-ghoona anha) does not merely mean they will
never physically be separated but that they will never become tired of
them. Their hunger for these heavens will never become overfull. Their
eyes will never tire from their beauty. These heavens will forever
contain sources of pleasure for them. They will stay attached to these
heavens from which they will neither be removed nor will they desire
to leave.
 
After this, Allah Almighty states, (Arabic text: kul lau kaanal
bahru…) O Muhammad (saw)! Proclaim that (Arabic text: lau kaaanal
bahru…) in order to write out the words (kalimaat) of my Lord, even if
the oceans were turned into ink, (Arabic Text: lanafida al-bahru) the
oceans would dry up but (Arabic Text: Kalimaatu rabi), my Lord’s words
(kalimaat) would never end (Arabic Text: wa lau ji naa bi misli hi
mada da) even if we brought identical oceans to replenish them. This
here is seemingly another subject.
 
And then seemingly a third subject is (Arabic Text: Kul inna ma ana
basharu mislu kum) that O Muhammad (saw), also proclaim that I too was
just a man like you. This can also mean that I am a man like you,
however based on a particular aspect of the subject matter, a more
correct translation is that proclaim that I too was merely a man like
you all, (Arabic Text: yuha ilayya) but see how revelation has
completely changed my fate. I arose from people just like you but
became a recipient of God’s revelations and then reached such a
magnificently high status. However, this blessing of God is not
restricted to me alone but it is an open invitation to all. It is a
general invitation, but then you will have to become like me -
whatever I have done, you will have to do exactly the same. And what
are those things that I have done? (Arabic Text: fa man kaana yarju
liqaa a rabihi…) If after seeing me, your heart too has desired that
it may achieve these lofty spiritual heights, and that you too may
attain communion with your Lord, then you also must perform righteous
deeds and hold no one as a partner with God. This is also seemingly a
separate subject and these three subjects seem to have no relevance
with one another. But if we ponder deeply over the subject matter of
the central verse, then the meaning of the preceding and the following
verses become abundantly clear.
 
Here, the Holy Quran is mentioning the words (kalimaat) of Allah
stating that God’s words (kalimaat) can never be exhausted. And this
chapter of the Holy Quran happens to be related to the refutation of
Christianity. Its introductory verses and its concluding verses relate
specifically to Christianity and various aspects of its refutation are
stated within it. Jesus (as) has been given the title of word
(kalimah) in the Holy Quran. It attests to the fact that he indeed was
a word (kalaam), but in what ways he was a word (kalaam) has not been
clarified. Christians take word (kalaam) in the sense that Jesus (as)
was a unique entity, an equal partner in Godhood, and he alone was the
word (kalaam) and there is none other who is a word (kalaam).

At another place in the Holy Quran, by stating (Arabic: bi kalimatin
minh) meaning a word from Him, it is further elaborated that God has
many words (kalimaat) and of these words (kalimaat), the Messiah (as)
was simply one of them. And God’s words (kalimaat) are never ending
and by applying the word “words” (kalimaat) in various ways, the Holy
Quran has shown that the subject of the word “word” (kalimah) is
extremely broad. Every revelation (kalaam) that is revealed on a
prophet is also considered “words” (kalimaat). Every righteous
personality who forms a bond with Allah almighty and then remains
steadfast, his spiritual branches extend into the heavens and after
receiving the favors of God, he eats of newly acquired spiritual fruit
and passes it on to the world. Such people too have been designated as
word (kalimah). So the question that would then arise is that if we
take the meaning of word (kalimah) to refer to human beings, then is
that limited to one person, two persons, or some other amount? Is it
the case that they used to exist and this status is no longer being
bestowed on anyone or will this process continue? Furthermore, word
(kalimah) applies to every portion of every revelation (kalaam) of God
and also applies to all the meanings derived from them.
 
The verse (Arabic text: lau kaanal bahru…) specifically also refers to
the Holy Quran whereas the Holy Quran can be written out, if not with
one then maybe two or three ink pots but a dozen ink pots at most. So
then what does it mean when it is said that if you start writing the
words (kalaam) of God, oceans will be dried up and even if those
oceans are replaced then they too will be dried up, but the words
(kalaam) of God will still not be exhausted? This could only mean that
every word (kalimah) of the Quran contains within it numerous words
(kalimaat) – they each contain the signs of Allah and cover vast
subjects. If we take the meaning of the word “words” (kalimaat) to
mean subjects, then the words (kalimaat) would become infinite.
 
After the revelation of God (kalaam-e-ilahi), prophets are also referred to as words (kalimaat) and not just Jesus (as) but rather every prophet was a word (kalimah) and all the righteous men of God are considered words (kalimaat). Therefore, the believers mentioned in the verse (Arabic text: innalazina aamanu…), meaning those that will be granted gardens in the heavens in which they will abide forever without ever tiring from them nor ever being ejected from them by God Almighty, these are the those very words (kalimaat) from whom some are elaborated upon in the following verses. In those verses, glad tidings are given to the Holy Prophet (saw) that the Christians are proud over having just one word (kalimah) and they believe him to be unique in that status - and We (God) undoubtedly called the Messiah (as) a word (kalimah) - but we are making you, Holy Prophet (saw), one through whom words are created (kalimah gar). Through you, Holy Prophet (saw), countless words (kalimaat) will come into being, and all those believers who are being promised infinite gardens of heaven, they will all be words (kalimaat) of God who will be ascribed to you. Thus, announce that my Lord’s words (kalimaat) can never be exhausted. Allah will bestow upon you, Holy Prophet (saw), pure words (kalimaat) in such vast quantity that their coming into being will never be halted and in each of such people, there will be oceans of meaning and oceans of virtue and righteous.
 
Why is this so? It is because it is a natural result of their obedience to the Holy Prophet (saw) that they are bestowed such a status. As such, in order to draw attention towards this point, in the third verse the Holy Prophet (saw) is made to announce that I am one through whom words are created (kalimah gar). I was a man just as you are, I was just a simple person like you, but when you form a bond with me, when you follow me and start to perform good deeds like the ones I have performed, and you attach yourself to the oneness of God the way I have firmly grasped on to the oneness of God, then the blessings of the revelation of God that makes one a word (kalimah) will also begin to be bestowed upon you, as I, the Holy Prophet (saw), have not come to limit this blessing to myself. I have in fact come to draw attention towards this blessing that you may see me and develop an interest and a love for this blessing, so that you develop a desire to form a bond with Allah Almighty and that as a result of this you do good works, follow me, and become pure in your belief in the oneness of God to whatever extent it is possible for you. Then see how the words (kalimaat) of God are limitless and these words (kalimaat) can never be finished. One of its meanings is also that the process of the endowment of blessings from God is a never-ending process.
 
Allah Almighty has also presented the Promised Messiah (as) as an example of a living embodiment of this verse in this day and age. The spiritual power of the Holy Prophet (saw) has also shown its effect in this day and age. And even in this day and age, the Holy Prophet’s (saw) spiritual strength has created one through whom words are created (kalimah gar). And the process of the creation of words that was once seemingly coming to an end, was reinstituted by Allah Almighty. Then as a result of the blessed company derived from the Promised Messiah (as), again many new words (kalimaat) started to be created. There are magnificent companions of the Promised Messiah (as), each of whom holds the status of word (kalimah). And each of their personalities is so deeply profound. Whether a normal person is able to recognize that profoundness or not, the reality is that gleaming within them is an undying hidden beauty - the love of Allah Almighty – which is a never-ending ocean. Often times these qualities remain hidden within them, and unless someone has been appointed by God and has been commanded to open up their personality to others, these types of people often quietly come and quietly move on and never become the center of people’s attention. And the process of words of God quietly passing through the world without being recognized, among other words (kalimaat) of God, is in and of itself a never-ending process.
 
The honorable and respected Hadhrat Chaudhry Muhammad Zafarullah Khan Sahib who passed away on September 1, I firmly believe that out of the many words (kalimaat) of God, he too was a word (kalimah). And he received a magnificently high status of righteousness from Allah. When I say this, I say it in the form of a prayer. And when the believers of God are commanded to speak in a positive manner about their deceased loved ones and elders, that too is to be done not in the form of a verdict but in the form of a prayer. This is because as far the final judgement is concerned, it is only up to God to determine virtue and righteousness. He alone is the knower of the unseen and the seen. He states, (Arabic Text: Fala tuzaku anfusakum…) (Al-Najm: 33) that you should neither claim to be righteous nor give verdicts about your friends and loved ones that they are certainly righteous. On the other hand, the Holy Prophet (saw) stated that you should speak of the deceased in good terms and in such a way that you give them the benefit of any doubt. But there cannot be any contradiction between these two. There is no contradiction between the words of God and the Holy Prophet (saw). The implication is only that you speak of your brothers, your elders, and your friends in a way that gives them the benefit of the doubt and in a positive manner – in such a way that you hope of God almighty that your estimations of them end up being true. And even if they are not true, then they form into a prayer for them. You should speak of them in such a way that Allah Almighty’s mercy may fall on them and the benefit of the doubt that you give them may turn into a reality for them.
 
So when I say that I have firm belief, I say this in a manner of prayer – I express it only as far as the extent of my knowledge [about the spirituality of Chaudhry Sahib (ra)] allows. However, as far as passing a verdict, neither you nor I possess that right and neither of us are (Arabic text: Aalimul ghaib washahad…) or knowers of the unseen or all seeing. However, as much as human faculties can gather, as much as could be gathered from observing him from afar, and observing him closely, I gained knowledge about him - he who was present and had lived a great portion of his life even before my birth and I pondered over that portion of his life that I found after reaching mature consciousness from childhood. I also analyzed the depths of his knowledge, studied his books, examined articles that authors wrote about him, listened to the impressions that righteous people of God had of him, and sometimes even got the opportunity to peer into such of his virtues that are usually hidden from the eyes of the general public. I also had the opportunity of exchanging letters with him and have seen him in such states that normally make one shy to express in front of people but whilst writing a letter one is able to reveal his innermost feelings. After analyzing all of these things, I have firm belief and I present my belief in front of God as a humble request that He may prove our belief to be true. That this very dear personality of ours, very pious friend of ours, who only a few days ago departed from this world leaving us sorrowful, may also be counted as righteous in the eyes of Allah, may also be looked upon by God with love and compassion, and that he may have appeared in front of his Lord being [pleased with Him and He pleased with Chaudhry Sahib (ra)] (Arabic Text: radhiatam mardhiyya).
 
He was a manifestation of this verse of the Holy Quran and he was truly among those servants of the Holy Prophet (saw) who in their own rights bore testimony to the fact that the Holy Prophet (saw) was certainly one through whom words are created (kalimah gar). The light that the Promised Messiah (as) received and the blessings he was endowed with were also a result of the mercy and blessings of the Holy Prophet (saw). And he [the Promised Messiah (as)] too was watered with the benefit of these blessings and was appointed to irrigate these blessings further. To that effect, he [the Promised Messiah (as)] too, in the deputyship of the Holy Prophet (saw), was made into one through whom words are created (kalimah gar). And the blessings that were awarded to Chaudhry Muhammad Zafarullah Khan Sahib glisten with the signs of truthfulness of the Promised Messiah (as). And of this fact, he [Chuadhry Sahib (ra)], was most cognizant - so intensely aware that it was always at the forefront of his mind.
 
I have assessed his personality from many different angles. His infatuation with the Promised Messiah (as) stemmed from the realization that the Promised Messiah (as) conferred such a favor upon him that had completely revolutionized his destiny – and this fact always remained on his mind. It was in England, that once a reporter from BBC One, while interviewing him asked out of the blue, what is the most important moment of your life? Without any hesitation or even taking a second to ponder, he quickly replied that the most important moment of my life was when I along with my mother appeared in front of the Promised Messiah (as), I took in his blessed face and I firmly placed my hands into his hands.
 
After that day, he never retracted his hand and continued to hold on to his hand. And all the venerations he received were because of that loyalty, steadfastness, and perseverance on the path of righteousness. And so it was that hand that he had given that was never withdrawn. And he always lived but as a subordinate to the commands of the Promised Messiah (as). In every field, in every field of academics, in every field of hard work and toil, in every field of self-reflection, the realization that I placed my hands in the hands of one appointed by Allah remained paramount for him. So as far as I am able and as far as God has enabled me, I will continue to fulfill the dictates of this realization. And by the grace and mercy of God, he fulfilled those dictates with extreme admirableness and extreme competence. And that prophecy of the Promised Messiah (as) was fulfilled in his favor that was revealed to him [the Promised Messiah (as)] repeatedly - and its repeated revelation was a sign of its abundant fulfillment that was given to him. The Promised Messiah (as) states:
“God has informed me again and again that He will grant me great glory and will instill my love in people’s hearts. He shall spread my Movement all over the world and shall make my sect triumphant over all other sects. The members of my sect shall so excel in knowledge and insight that they will confound everyone with the light of their truth, and by the strength of their arguments and signs. Every nation will drink of this fountain, and this Movement will spread and blossom until it rapidly encompasses the entire world. Many tribulations and obstacles shall come, but God will remove them all and will fulfill His promise. God addressed me and said: 'I shall grant thee blessing upon blessing until kings shall seek blessings from thy garments!’” 
(Divine Manifestations pg. 24 (Tajalliyat-e-Ilahiyyah, Ruhani Khazain vol. 20 pg. 409))
 
This prophecy has continued to be fulfilled in many ways and in the form of many persons but the late Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib specifically was able to fulfill this prophecy in a way that by the light of his truthfulness and via his arguments and signs, he would often literally leave everyone speechless and confounded. This was the case in the field of politics, the field of law, and also in the field of preaching. He achieved such a great status of representation that our members aside, even the opponents would spontaneously announce that this champion has left others dumbfounded.
 
Other than preaching, the services that he was able to render for the sake of religion included fighting such sensitive legal cases of the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community, which he handled very admirably and followed their proceedings with extreme care, that often times were such tough legal charges from which it seemed impossible to be liberated. Such was the case that at times, for some individuals, it seemed as if they were completely entrapped by their legal cases, however with great wisdom, with masterful oratory, with great competency he fulfilled the rights of representation in those cases and won magnificent accolades in this field.
 
Then in the world of politics, Allah almighty enabled him to perform great services serve in great capacities. His advocacy on behalf of India in front of the British government will also always be written in history in gold and any historian who possesses even an ounce of righteousness and honesty will be unable to disregard the services rendered by Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib (ra). There were many other instances where he served, such as the Round Table Conferences, and when I had a list compiled it became quite lengthy. Hence, it is not possible to mention all aspects of his broad, lengthy, and accomplished life in this short sermon. I am just briefly mentioning some aspects in an effort to engender prayers and as a reminder to inspire you so that from among you people of this caliber can rise.
 
The point I would like to make is that the verses that I have recited, they present infinite avenues of advancement. It is impossible to conceptualize anyone greater than the blessed personality of the Holy Prophet (saw). In these verses, it is said to the Holy Prophet (saw) that after making mention of yourself and the fact that your God is bestowing revelation upon you, give everyone an open invitation and tell them that if you have the fortitude, then come and adopt the paths upon which I excelled, hold fast to my example, and follow in my footsteps. These paths are infinite and no one is barred from adopting them. There are no artificial boundaries that can prove unattainable for you, so you are free to excel, and you have an invitation to excel.
 
However, in order to excel you will have to pursue the paths that the Holy Prophet (saw) traversed. There is an open invitation to advance towards the Holy Prophet (saw) and there is no limit set on the possibilities. However, it has been very clearly established that it is impossible for anyone to follow in the footsteps of the Holy Prophet (saw) but then surpass him. Nonetheless, this doesn’t come as a prohibition but as an invitation. The Holy Prophet’s (saw) status being termed the pinnacle of spiritual height is not used to cause despair but rather it is used as an open invitation and motivation. As for all those junior to the rank of the Holy Prophet (saw), the possibility of following in their ways and excelling beyond them even exists.
 
Thus, the followers of the Holy Prophet (saw) are given the wonderful glad tidings that if even the greatness of the Holy Prophet (saw) is not restricting you from excelling and striving on the paths of righteousness, then how can you perceive his small and humble servants to be at a ranks that are not achievable? How could you despair and say that such and such has reached such spiritual heights that we cannot exceed them. The Holy Quran states that this is a wide-open pathway and as far as becoming words (kalimaat) is concerned, the Holy Prophet (saw) did not come to create just a few. He was not such a personality who would only leave behind a few words (kalimaat) or the Blessed Ten (*Ashrah Mubashrah – Ten companions of the Holy Prophet (saw) who were given the promise of paradise during their lifetime). The ability to produce words (kalimaat) bestowed on the Holy Prophet (saw) is endless and can be benefitted from as long as you continue to fulfill your rights concerning it.
 
(Arabic Text: Kul lau kana bahru… )
O Muhammad, proclaim that those words (kalimaat) being granted to me by my Lord are so vast and the powers of God almighty are so limitless – here words (kalimaat) also means powers – and God has such limitless treasures that if you truly become deserving of them, they can never be exhausted. Hence, infinite avenues have been opened for you to excel.
 
The reason I am mentioning this is that while on one hand it may generate a prayer in your heart, on the other hand you may keep the prophecy of the Promised Messiah (as) that I read out to you in mind, that you may ponder over that fountain of the blessings of God almighty which was granted the name Muhammad (saw) from the heavens, and that you ponder over that source of blessings that contains words (kalimaat) that are never ending and is called the Holy Quran, and thereby you never let any thoughts of despair enter your hearts.
 
Remove the notion from your hearts that since one Zafarullah Khan is departing from us so all avenues of future Zafarullah Khan’s rising are closed. The Promised Messiah (as) has been given abundant and repeated glad tidings of incredible followers who will forever continue to come forth and when one departs another will step forward to take his place. You should raise your morale. Adopt those paths of righteousness that Hadhrat Chaudhry Sahib continued to adopt. Become adorned with the quality of loyalty the way he was so amply adorned with it, develop that courage and steadfastness that was a special part of his character, and become lost in the love and affection of Allah the almighty just as Allah had granted Chaudhry Sahib special aspects of His love – nay strive to dive to even greater depths than him. As for the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community, no avenues of advancement can ever be closed. After any particular person’s demise, there is none who can say that now another like him will not rise again. There is only one the likes of whom the world has never seen, will never see, nor is another like him conceivable and that is our beloved master the Holy Prophet (saw). Despite being unique in his status, he (saw) was granted the characteristics of producing infinite words (kalimaat). Thus, you too should try to become one of these words (kalimaat).
 
The various aspects of Chaudhry Sahib’s personality that I wanted to mention turned out to be so vast that of them I had to select just a few, and even the few that I selected, it seems impossible to do justice to them in this small gathering. God almighty had granted him such magnificence that all the offices he held were always made to seem small in front of him and none of those offices could make him seem small. His personality was full of forbearance and was expansive. There was never an office he held that made it seem as if his status was raised because of it, but in fact it was always he who would raise the status of that office. He kept raising the stature of those offices so much so that when he vacated them, they would seem to be even higher in status than before – and this quality is granted to man as a result of humility.
 
If one ponders deeply, the\n one will discover that humility and having a deep capacity for forbearance are simply two names for the same thing. An ignorant and unwise person whose understanding is cursory imagines high status and expansiveness to be attained by raising one’s chin with haughtiness. However, one who understands human nature - who has learned the secrets of human nature from the Holy Quran - understands this truth well that within humility in fact is eminence and expansiveness and both of these characteristics are taught to us by each prescribed unit of our five daily prayers. First, we express humility while bowing and there we recite (Arabic text: subhana rabbi al azeem). Here, God almighty turns our attention towards the expanses by stating that since you have bowed you will receive expansiveness because you have bowed before the Lord of Expansiveness. The other movement that we make towards humbleness, which is the height of humility within the positions of prayer, is prostration. There, God almighty teaches us (Arabic text: subhana rabi al alaa x2) - that you have prostrated, but you have prostrated towards eminence because you have prostrated before (Arabic text: subhana rabbi al alaa) the Lord of Eminences.
 
Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib, practically, was very familiar with both of these points and their hidden secrets. Hence, his high statuses and eminences were both achieved as a result of humility. He had within him an unrelenting fervor for the service of religion and no worldly office could prevent him from carrying out his services. Regardless, he never even considered himself as extraordinary because of any worldly office anyway because he always considered all worldly offices as insignificant in his eyes as opposed to the service of faith seeming insignificant. Verily, the humility that I am referring to is the humility of one well acquainted with Allah. As such, it was in the service of religion where he believed his distinction to be and it was in service of religion where all his nobilities lied.
 
There is a remarkable incident in the history of India that in 1941 when Hadhrat Zafarullah Khan Sahib was appointed the judge of the Indian Federal Court of Justice, Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra) started an initiative calling people to present themselves to go preach in Mudhafaat-e-Qadian (small villages around Qadian). Side by side with other missionaries, a Justice of the Federal Court in 1941 and 1942, went around these small villages to preach and never once did it cross his mind that I have such a high rank and status, what will people say when they see or hear of what I am doing here. There were many small and backwards villages such as Depai, Bheeni, and Athwaal in which he went as a simple servant of Ahmadiyyat and with great pride and with this understanding that this work is a blessing and good fortune that I am receiving from Allah the almighty. This desire was not, however, simply for services that can easily be rendered under normal circumstances but rather the same deep burning desire to serve even in harsh and unsafe environments flared in his heart.
 
When Hadhrat Nematullah Khan was martyred in Kabul in 1924, Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra) called for the names of those who, after fully recognizing the dangers, would pledge to go to Kabul and carry out the work in place of the martyr that he could no longer execute as a result of his martyrdom, and they will not allow for a break in the continuity of the work. This was the essence of Hadhrat Musleh Maud’s (ra) call. At the time the names were presented, Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan (ra) was the president (ameer) of the Ahmadiyya Community of Lahore, and out of all the names presented, his name was at the top of the list which was published in Al-Fazal.
 
When presenting his name, he wrote a letter to Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra) in which, despite it being against his nature, which he himself mentions in the letter, because he was writing to his Imam, it was comparatively easier for him to fully express his inner feelings. This letter serves as an opportunity to peer into his true personality. What paths of righteousness was he traversing? What was his state of being emotionally and mentally? When he presented his name, he then looks at his life with scrutiny and assesses his inner intentions out of fear to make sure that god forbid might I be doing this in an effort to show off to others? You will see all of these thoughts reflected in this letter.
He writes:

“My master, my leader, may peace and blessings and the mercy of Allah be upon you. My life to this day has passed such that other than regret and sorrow, I have achieved nothing.” 
 
He was living a very successful life. He had already entered into politics, his competencies were becoming well known throughout India, and he had started to draw the attention of the intelligentsia of the Muslims of the country. Despite leading such a successful life, what is being expressed here is his own assessment of what he believed the sum and substance was of what he had gained and what status he saw himself to have achieved.
 
“...other than regret and sorrow, I have achieved nothing. I often ponder that is this even a life in which other than earning a living, one has no time to spare for anything else...”

He is writing this despite wholeheartedly fulfilling all of the responsibilities of the office of president (amarat) of the Ahmadiyya Community of Lahore at the time.
 
“...and that a person remain entrapped in worldly affairs, engrossed in various types of sins. Today, the news of the fortunate Hadhrat Nematulluah Khan Sahib’s death and meeting with his True Lover (God) came. Where in my heart there developed an extreme anguish, the thought also occured that this is your chance to bring your wasteful life to some use by presenting yourself for the service of Truth in the land of Afghanistan. Then I suddenly paused and pondered, that god forbid is this is a mere desire of my ego to show off to others out of the belief that I should just present my name because I will not actually be sent. And I went over in my mind all the difficulties and trials that would present themselves in this task and I challenged myself that since immediate martyrdom is a blessing that is not granted to everyone, so god forbid are you simply presenting yourself so that you can attain the status of a martyr as soon as you arrive and escape the worries of the world? Or do you in fact have within you the determination to live for a long period of time and sacrifice each day in the way of Allah and not turn away from this daily martyrdom? Dearest Huzur, I am weak, lazy, and value comfort, but after pondering my conscience tells me that not for the sake of showing off, not for immediate martyrdom, and not for the sake of escaping the worries of the world but for the sake of availing the opportunity to repent for my sins, to amass a spiritual treasure for my hereafter, and to seek the pleasure of Allah almighty I present myself for this service. If Allah almighty renders this service from a worthless and sinful person like me and enables me to spend the remaining days of my life in seeking His pleasure, then I seek no greater blessing or happiness than that. Huzur I am not an essayist and in the presence of Huzur, neither speech proves helpful nor is the pen, just as a poet has said:

Being passionate about something requires actions without words; 			Mere speeches in front of one’s beloved are of no avail.
(This is a couplet from the poetry of Hasrat Mohani which he has quoted.)

This is why I will stop here by saying that whenever Huzur gives the command, I am ready to head to Afghanistan and I seek the prayers of Huzur and the pleasure of Allah alone.

With peace
Huzur’s most humble servant
Sincerely, Zafarullah Khan
November 8, 1924”
 
This was his humility and this was his passion for service and his love for God. In actuality, it was the fear of the displeasure of God almighty that always remained on his mind and the desire to attain the love of God almighty - these very two motivations - that kept Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib advancing from one stage to the next throughout his life. This was the power that advanced him his entire life. His source of energy was the love of Allah almighty and whoever attains this source of energy, the doors of infinite blessings are opened by Allah almighty for them and they are granted guidance from God almighty at every step of the way. The fear of God is in the sense of recognizing the danger of being deprived of the love of God and the desire for attaining the love of Allah almighty is in the sense that we be able to perform such deeds that Allah almighty begin to look towards us with love and care. These are the two basic powers from which a believer attains every advancement, it is with the same strength that his prayers are exalted, his works are enhanced and elevated, and all his efforts and undertakings bear fruit. The believer is saved from dangers and is granted extraordinary signs of the support of God almighty. And the various manifestations of sacrificing in the way of God, be it sacrificing your wealth, be it sacrificing your time, sacrificing your life, or sacrificing your honor, in reality, it is these very two motivations which we call righteousness and it is this righteousness from which all these good deeds stem. Hence, God almighty revealed to the Promised Messiah (as), “If this root (i.e. righteousness) remains, then everything has remained” (Akhbar al-Hakam: August 31, 1901 / Malfoozat Vol. 1 pg, 536). When the Promised Messiah (as) was composing this couplet, he said the initial verse, “The root of every good deed is righteousness,” and this was such a beautiful point of spiritual understanding that before the Promised Messiah (as) was able to compose a second verse of the couplet, Allah almighty revealed it to him: “If this root (i.e. righteousness) remains, than everything has remained.”
 
As far as I have analyzed, Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib (ra) remained so firmly attached to the root of righteousness his entire life that the subject of (Arabic text: Asluha sabitun) (Ibrahim: 25) comes forth. In terms of his roots he was strong, steadfast and loyal. He would remain true to whatever he said. There was no duplicity in his character such as saying one thing and doing another - this type of contradiction was completely absent from his personality.
 
Thus after reaching this point, the services he was able to carry out were simply a natural result and they require no further investigation or explanation; whoever can attain these two attributes i.e. the sum and substance of righteousness in which there is a fear of losing the love of God and a desire to attain His love, for them all other matters become easy. People observing from the outside conclude that such a person is making great sacrifices, exerting a lot of effort, and at every step his wishes and desires remain unfulfilled, and the person is entangled in adversities and sorrows. However, such a person himself knows the inner reality that it was just those first two things that were difficult. In reality, truly understanding the meaning righteousness and then clutching on to it is the most difficult phase. After that, all other stages of advancement become easy.     

He had an astounding and extensive life so much so that when a few days ago a member of parliament came to me to express his condolences on the passing of Chaudhry Sahib, I said to him, you are sympathizing over just one person, when in fact he did not live his life simply as one person. Within him lived many different personalities and at once he lived multiple lives. And with God’s grace, he lived a long period of time continuing to fully exhibit within himself multiple personalities. Some perceived him only to be an apathetic politician throughout their whole lives and they saw him as such because he had held a very successful and busy career. However, some also saw him as an extremely kind hearted and sympathetic person. Such a person who did not have the strength to bear the plight of the poor with indifference. One who, despite having great fortitude did not have the capacity to witness human suffering and walk by apathetically. In these aspects, his threshold for sensitivity for the pain of others was extremely low, as if down to the ground. In religious terminology and Islamic terminology, humility also means that as far as the pains of mankind are concerned, you should lower your threshold of sensitivity so that you too can become a Mercy for Mankind (rehmatul lil alameen) [a title for the Holy Prophet Muhammad (saw)]. All the blessings attained in this path are achieved by lowering this threshold. 
 
Accordingly, what is striking is that aside from his various other responsibilities, if anyone began to simply enumerate his humanitarian work alone, I don’t believe there is a single person at this time who can claim that I have accounted for all of his services. This is because many of his humanitarian services were performed openly and many discreetly and his services were so widespread in various initiatives that at this time, realistically, it is simply impracticable for anyone to account for all of them. Mention of them haven’t made it into any documents and neither can they be found in any kind of trusts, including those that he himself established. He helped the needy so extensively that when by chance I came across such accounts, at that time the extent of it would become apparent to me. What I mean by this is that there were some such needy people who themselves came and mentioned that we were stuck in such and such difficulty and all we did was but send a message to Chaudhry Sahib and after that we had no further worries about the matter. He helped the destitute, orphans, poor laborers, poor farmers, people afflicted with paralysis, some immersed in various ailments, and budding students who were poor such that he performed humanitarian services for various types of needy people. And not only this but he generously donated to many organizations such that it appears as though he spent his entire life savings in this work.  
 
And when you glance at his financial contributions and services to the
Jama’at, it seems as if all of his earnings are being spent on the
Jama’at and nowhere else. He contributed to the Jama’at with immense
generosity and with open hands. At this time, all the buildings of the
London mission are completely as a result of his personal efforts and
all expenses paid for by his personal finances. The mission house,
this hall, the smaller hall for the ladies, and the guest house, all
of these by the grace of God were a result of his efforts. And for
himself, he kept a very small room. In that alone he would spend his
time and even that did not utilize towards the end of his life. When
it was needed by the Jama’at towards the end of his life or perhaps
because he could not climb too many stairs, either way he left even
that for the Jama’at.

In addition to this, he wholeheartedly participated in various
initiatives that periodically arose. Specifically, during the time of
Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra) an initiative calling for individuals to
pledge all of their assets was made, he was amongst the first in
offering up all his assets. The way he had righteousness and virtue
instilled within him shows he was a man of great magnificence. This is
also evident from the letter that I read out to you. When he devoted
(waqf) his life, what he meant by it was that he will not even keep a
penny for himself and he was fully prepared to give everything. The
type of dangers for which Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra) had preemptively
launched the initiative to call people to pledge all of their assets
never presented themselves. So I am aware that the pledge for all
assets was never collected but regardless of that, whenever and for
how much ever a need arose Chaudhry Sahib never felt any hesitation
even for a moment, but in fact preferred that Hadhrat Musleh Maud (ra)
himself assess and take any amount needed as if it were his own. This
was his attitude in regards to his financial sacrifices for the
Jama’at which always remained such.
 
His extensive political life is so broad that it is not at all
possible to mention all his services. The favors that Allah almighty
enabled him to confer upon whole nations were a manifestation of the
revelations vouchsafed to the Promised Messiah (as) which include the
words that, others too will benefit extensively from the blessings
brought with the Promised Messiah (as) and every nation will drink
from this spiritual fountain.

Allah almighty raised him to such a high status where every nation did
in fact drink from this fountain. This status was that of the
presidency of the United Nations and if that period of the United
Nations history were to be remembered by any one characteristic, then
it would be known as the period of morality for the United Nations. He
established all Islamic and moral values there. And that was such a
time when even atheist politicians who took part in the proceedings of
the United Nations, sat respectfully and calmly would never engage in
immoral speech. Booing, over-dramatization, using words of contempt,
and losing self-control in fits of anger were not be found in the
United Nations at that time. His practice was such that he would
regularly recite from the Holy Quran and would draw teachings from it
during the proceedings. He would continually impart moral teachings
without regard for whether anyone accepted these teachings from him or
the Holy Quran or not. God almighty endowed him with such courage and
such magnificence in his speech as a result of his character - as
magnificence in speech to the extent that even strangers become
awestruck comes from one’s character and not oratory.
 
On one occasion while having frank conversation over dinner, Chaudhry
Sahib himself mentioned to me that he was amazed to see that such
people who had no connection to religion, had no connection to faith,
God almighty had compelled them to treat me well and with
respect. Because he understood everything to be a favor of Allah
almighty upon him, he would always mention things in this manner -
that it was the special blessings and favors of Allah almighty that He
placed a sort of an awe over their hearts compelling them to accept
what I told them even though, on the face of it, I had no right to
enforce such moral rules within the proceedings.

The status of a president is well known and well understood, but in
the world of politics, the expectations we have about the authority a
president holds do not actually pan out. There were representatives
from such free and unrestrained countries that in a moment of fury
they would forget all manners. Regard for who might be presiding,
discipline and order, and even adherence to punctuality was the extent
to which he had to teach manners there. And without a doubt, at that
time the words of the Promised Messiah (as) were also fulfilled in the
form of one person. Of course there are numerous Ahmadis through whom
nations have benefited, however there in the United Nations, all of
the characteristics came together in one person. That one spiritual
fountain who used to take pride in being a servant of the Promised
Messiah (as) watered and benefited all the nations of the world.
 
Allah almighty granted him such great opportunities to render
tremendous services to various nations in a time when modern history
was taking shape. The foundations of modern history as we know it
today were being laid down at that time. In such a time period, Allah
almighty granted him the opportunity to appear before the United
Nations first as a member, then as a representative, and finally as
its president. Hence, from the time he became the foreign minister of
Pakistan to the time he became the president of the United Nations,

He had the opportunity to serve the Arab people on the matter of
Palestine and he rendered such great services that notable Arab

stand up and step forward to kiss his hands and would take full pride
in it. They had extreme love and affection for him and those among

not just the matter of Palestine. He had the opportunity to serve

their rights. Among the Muslim countries he also had the opportunity
to serve Indonesia.
 
Two years ago, after much effort, I was able to acquire a record of

Muzaffar Ahmad Sahib I was able to obtain the entire record. I

Sahib could be presented to the world from time to time because some

claims. For example, where he served Muslim countries, there exist

a humble man of God who selflessly served many nations and

I had been mentioning my personal impressions and would like return to

other extensively and I know that he was extremely meek of heart, had
the fear of Allah almighty, and was humbled by Him. I also had the
opportunity to offer prayers alongside him. Whenever I would visit
Lahore, he would always lovingly invite me and it was never possible
that he be present in Lahore and I return home without meeting with
him, or having a meal with him, or at least spending some time with
him. This was because I did not have the ability to bear any objection
of his against me and he would most certainly object if I left without
meeting him. And when we would offer prayers together, he would
request me to lead. Observing him closely, the spiritual condition he
would be in during prayer was not a question of simply hearing him but
rather it would be something one would begin to experience. There was
a wondrous humility and lowliness in his prayers. Each and every word
he would recite he presented to God almighty adorned like a pearl - as
if fully understanding the subject of (Arabic: attahiyyatu lillah), he
knew that his worship would only be accepted if he presented it as a
gift, otherwise it would be meaningless.
 
I had the opportunity to learn even more about his personality from
many other ways such as conversations during private gatherings and
from writing letters back and forth, as I have mentioned before. I
have saved many of his letters very securely and they were such that
because it was against his nature that people come to know his
internal emotions, there were only some few friends with whom he would
open up. In his letters, his humility was so incredible that it would
make the reader embarrassed. It was because of his humility and
sincerity which knew no bounds that Allah almighty had created a
special bond between us.  It is no surprise that after the start of my
Khilafat, the first vision (kashaf) shown to me by God almighty was in
which Chaudhry Zafarullah Khan Sahib was shown. It was an amazing
vision that left me astounded because a person’s mind generally cannot
go out that far and think of such things. It was one or two days into
my Khilafat that someone asked, have you received any revelations or
visions etcetera since becoming the Khalifa? I replied, so far I have
not received any and I am simply moving along and however God almighty
is dealing with me is fine. A few days after this, following Fajr
prayer, I experienced a vision in which I saw very clear that Chaudhry
Zafarullah Khan Sahib is lying down and is conversing with Allah
almighty and though I am listening to the conversation, I am
distant. I am aware that he is lying in London, but it is similar to
the way proximity is sometimes depicted in movies in which someone is
having a telephone conversation somewhere far off but one is still
able to hear the whole conversation - such special effects are
possible with various film techniques. So in this vision, I was seeing
that Chaudhry Sahib is lying in his bed and conversing with Allah
almighty and while I am listening in my mind I am also commenting on
the conversation but my voice is unable to reach them. Allah almighty
asked Chaudhry Sahib how much work do you have left? Chaudhary Sahib
replied, I have four years of work left but if you grant me even one
year then it would be sufficient. Hearing this gave me a sort of a
shock and I wished to say to Chaudhary Sahib that you should ask God
almighty for four years, what are you saying that even if you are
granted one year it would be sufficient? He is asking the all-powerful
God and is stating that he has four years of work left but then says
that just one year is enough. This caused me angst but as I have
mentioned, during this vision I could not extend my voice to them - I
could only listen to the conversation taking place.
 
The following day I wrote this vision and sent it to Chaudhry Hameed
Nasrullah Sahib and his wife but it worried me that it may be the case
that God almighty may grant a relatively longer life but Chaudhry
Sahib may only be able to carry out his work for one year of it. And
that is exactly how it panned out. In 1983, he suffered a severe bout
of his ailment after which he was forced to gradually separate himself
from his work such that he was only really able to work to his full
capacity for one additional year and practically all the work he was
able to complete would be up to that point. Then because of his
ailment he had to return to Pakistan and after that his health
continued to decline and got weaker to a point that all he was able to
do was read and study. Admittedly, he was not given four years after
that, but within this too is the wonderous majesty of Allah
Almighty. Two years after I had this vision, he suffered another
severe bout of his ailment in February of 1982 about which the doctors
said that there is no chance of survival. At that time I was in
Karachi, I was informed that a call has come from Lahore expressing

by God Almighty regarding Chaudhry Sahib’s ailment. My eyes remain
fixated on one sentence alone the gist of which is that I give life
and I also relieve burdens and make provisions such that the subject
of giving life (yuhyi - with a ḥa) and also with the other ‘ha’ that I
make provisions (muhayya - with a ha). From that, it occured to me

His savings had been seized by a usurper and it appeared that there was no way of regaining the amount. The usurper claimed that he had suffered a great loss and that he could not repay the amount. Some believed that he was just making excuses and was taking advantage of Chaudhry Sahib’s simple nature. When I had my final meeting with him, even then he felt extremely burdened because of it. The content of the dream gave me the assurance that God willing, Allah Almighty would not cause him do die in such a state and would certainly keep him alive until the burden of paying the remaining pledged amount is lifted.

And thus Allah Almighty granted him an extraordinarily extended life. During this time there were many critical stages and severe bouts of his ailment and each time the doctors deemed that in their estimation there was no hope for survival but each time Allah Almighty would save him with His divine grace.

Then I moved here to London and Allah Almighty enabled me to help with regards to the amount that had been usurped that everyone had written off as unobtainable. God Almighty also sent my maternal uncle’s son, Rafiuddin Esq. from Karachi to London to help in this case. He is a very talented lawyer and an expert negotiator in these matters. Hence the proceedings took place and through his and his firm’s legal expertise he was able to corner the usurper and obtain a repayment of just over £200,000 and this was the very amount that had Chaudhry Sahib worried. Only a few months ago Chaudhry Sahib told me, that all praise belongs to Allah that it is not a matter that we will receive that amount that’s greater than £200,000 but by the grace of Allah it is already in our possession. Since in the dream both of the glad tidings of “giving life” and “making provisions” were mentioned together, I felt an immediate sort of mild shock that now one portion, that is “making provisions,” has been completed and I worried about the other portion. However, God continued to grant him life and did not call him to Himself until he entered the fourth year. All four years were not given but he was in the third month of the fourth year and Allah Almighty called him back to Himself.

But when Allah treats a person such that He begins to show signs of that person’s righteousness to others and begins to show signs of His love for him, then the hope or the expectation that Allah will treat that person with love and compassion enters a totally different realm.

Translator’s Note

Hadhrat Khalifatul Masih IV (ra) eulogizing sermon following the demise of Sir Chaudhary Zafarullah Khan (ra), an esteemed companion of the Promised Messiah (as), the English translation of which is being presented here. The first word that needs explanation here is the Urdu word “Sahib” (صاحب). In English language the correct translation is “sir” and for due respect the name would become Sir Chaudhary Zafrullah Khan. However, unlike English, in Urdu the word “sahib” is applied after the name and in this case the name would be Chaudhary Zafrullah Khan Sahib. With use of the word Sahib at the end of the name, there is no need to say the whole name. First, Middle, or last name with the word Sahib at the end will offer due respect. In this translation the word Sahib has been kept in its original form due to the context in which it has been used in. Examples of this are “Choudhary Sahib”, or “Khan Sahib” which will be noticed in the translation.
 
The second and perhaps the most important word in this translation
that needs explanation is the term “Kalima” (کلمہ) which literally
means “word” and “Kalimaat” (کلمات) which is the plural and means
“words”. This word has many meanings and among them are words that are
written out and also signs in various forms including
personalities. During this translation, the word “Kalima” or
“kalimaat” has been used many times. In the earlier portion of the
sermon the “Kalima” is being referred to words as they are written
out. Further in the translation the alternate meaning of “kalima” has
been implied which is “a sign of God” For those who are not familiar
with the early days use of the term “word”; it has been used in
context of sign. In the Bible, Jesus (as) has been called Word of God,
meaning a sign of God. In this translation wherever this term has been
used, first is the transliteration as “Kalima” followed by its meaning
“Word” which is being used as a noun.
